Birdy Hop - Unity - 2018

My mentor told me that it's easy to start creating something, but it's hard to complete it, upon hearing that I want to finally start, complete and ship out a game myself; this is Birdy Hop.

Inspired by Flappy Bird, Birdy Hop is a simple ketchapp game where you control a bird hopping from platform to platform gaining as many points as possible.

One of the more challenging experience in developing Birdy Hop was the game balancing, is it too hard? too easy? I wanted an experience where newcomers would get around 10 points, whereas skilled player could get 50+ points. After a few iterations with a different testers on Android and iOS, I believe I chose the correct settings that fullfilled my goals.

Knots - SpriteKit - 2017

Knots - A ketchapp game developed for the iOS platform where the player controls the lighthouse to save as many ships as possible from crashing into the middle

While learning to develop iOS applications in Swift and Objective C, I stumbled upon the Apple game development kit called SpriteKit and decided to make a game within a week.

There were a few features that SpriteKit had compared to Unity that made development a lot faster and smoother such as their animation api. An example: If I wanted a gameobject to go from one point to another on Unity I would probably put coordinates and if statements inside the Update(){..} function. However in SpriteKit, it's just the simple class func move(to location: CGPoint, duration: TimeInterval) -> SKAction.

At the end of the day, each game engines have their own pros and cons.

Platformation - Unity - 2018

This was my 3rd global game jam and it was an amzing experience filled with new friends and new knowledge.

I decided to go with developers whom I have never worked with and don't know very well, so I ended up teaming up with few of the executives of AGDA (AMS Game Development Association)

Turned out they were co-ops working at East Side Games and I learnt quite a lot from them.

We were able to complete the MVP of the game which is a local multiplayer couch co-op platfomer inspired by Ultimate Chicken Horse

One player would control the jumper who tries to jump to the red flag, and the rest of the players would have a chance to shoot at the jumper

If the jumper gets shot, the body turns into an obstacle that stop or helps the jumper along the way. Everyone then takes turns being the jumper

We were able to present it at the game jam and I couldn't be more proud with my team.

Speed Touch - Unity - 2017

I had an idea and a vision for a game, where you guide your finger through a fast moving maze trying to survive as long as possible to unlock the next level which gets even faster.

I wanted the same addictive game experience I had playing "Super Hexagon", with the minimalistic art and techno beat in the background.

Unforunately this proof of concept quickly fell apart when I realized that the Android touch had a significant lag time when detecting it's current position when dragging your finger accross the screen of at least 0.5 seconds. It made for quite a lot of unfair and unintuitive losses for the player.

If the technology was a lot more smoother, I may have kept working on this game since I had some art made and the music choice chosen
